# Career Development Plans

This file contains two career development plans, one for Alex Chen and one for Jordan Martinez. They sit at different places on their career journey — Alex is one step away from Tech Lead, and Jordan is still building towards Senior Engineer. Both plans are honest about the current state, realistic about timelines, and concrete about the support needed from me as manager.

I want to say one thing up front about both plans: a career plan is not a contract. It is a living document. Alex and Jordan each get a copy, we review it together every month, and we change it when reality changes. If I write a plan in March and never look at it again, it is useless by June.

---

## Career Plan 1 — Alex Chen

**Current role:** Senior Software Engineer
**Target role:** Tech Lead
**Timeline:** 12 months (Q3 2025 formal conversation, Q4 2025 role change if ready)

### Career aspirations (in Alex's own words)

Alex has been clear about where they want to go. I wrote this down during our third 1:1 so I would not paraphrase:

- **Short-term (12 months):** become Tech Lead on this team or another backend team
- **Medium-term (3 years):** step into an Engineering Manager role
- **Long-term (5+ years):** lead engineering at an early-stage startup

Alex's long-term goal matters here. A lot of managers skip the long-term question because it feels too distant to plan for. I think it is the most important question — it tells me whether Alex is building towards a people-heavy path or a technical-heavy path. The EM goal means we should be investing in people and communication skills, not just architecture depth.

### Current state — honest assessment

I graded Alex against the Tech Lead competency framework we use on the engineering side. I was honest — not generous, not harsh.

**Strengths (where Alex is already at or above the Tech Lead bar):**

| Skill | Level | Evidence |
|---|---|---|
| Code quality | Expert | Every review Alex does is a teaching moment for the rest of the team |
| System design | Advanced | The API redesign doc is the best piece of design writing on this team |
| Technical mentoring | Proficient, trending Advanced | Casey's growth rate is the clearest evidence |
| Incident response | Proficient | Led 3 P1s cleanly in H1 |
| Code review culture | Advanced | Alex's PR comments are patient and specific |

**Gaps (where Alex needs to grow to be ready):**

| Skill | Level | Why it matters for Tech Lead |
|---|---|---|
| Stakeholder management | Developing | This is the biggest gap. A Tech Lead is judged on how Product and Design feel about working with engineering |
| Project planning | Proficient, need Advanced | Needs to get comfortable with longer horizons than one sprint |
| Meeting facilitation | Developing | A Tech Lead runs the room, not just the code |
| Strategic thinking | Proficient | Needs practice at "what should we build next?" not just "how do we build it?" |
| Delegation | Developing | Alex still takes the hardest piece personally. Tech Leads delegate to grow others |

### Development plan

The plan is split into 4 quarters. Each quarter has two or three specific goals, concrete actions, and measurable success criteria. I deliberately did not overload it — 3 quarters of moderate focus beats 1 quarter of trying to fix everything.

#### Quarter 1 (July – September): Foundation

**Focus:** close the biggest gap — stakeholder management — by giving Alex real exposure to Product and Design.

**Goals:**
1. Build working relationships with at least 3 non-engineering stakeholders
2. Lead one cross-team project end-to-end
3. Start shadowing a Tech Lead from a peer team

**Actions:**

| Action | Support I will give | Timeline | How we measure it |
|---|---|---|---|
| Lead the B2B platform API design | Intros to Priya (PM) and Dinesh (Design lead) | Month 1 | Design doc approved by architecture council |
| Attend weekly Product planning meetings as the eng rep | Calendar invite + briefing on what to expect | Ongoing | Alex comfortable contributing by week 4 |
| Shadow Tech Lead meetings on the platform team | Intro to Sandun (platform TL) | Month 1–3 | 3 shadow sessions in the quarter |
| Practice a stakeholder update weekly | Template + feedback from me | Weekly | Alex owns this without reminders by month 2 |

**Success metrics at the end of Q1:**
- 1 cross-team project delivered
- 3 named stakeholder relationships
- 3 completed shadow sessions with written reflections

#### Quarter 2 (October – December): Strategic growth

**Focus:** strategic thinking and meeting facilitation. Alex should start owning more of the "what" and less of only the "how."

**Goals:**
1. Demonstrate strategic input on the team roadmap
2. Improve meeting facilitation with real reps
3. Present externally for the first time

**Actions:**

| Action | Support I will give | Timeline | How we measure it |
|---|---|---|---|
| Co-write the team's H1 2026 roadmap with me | Weekly working sessions | Month 4–5 | Roadmap shipped, Alex's voice visible in it |
| Facilitate the weekly architecture review | I sit in the back, give feedback after | Month 4 onwards | Alex improves between sessions 1 and 4 |
| Deliver an internal tech talk on the API redesign | Review of the deck and rehearsal time | Month 5 | Talk delivered to 20+ people |
| Write one architecture proposal from scratch | Guidance on framing, review before circulation | Month 6 | Proposal circulated and discussed |

**Success metrics at the end of Q2:**
- Roadmap shipped with Alex as co-author
- 8 team meetings facilitated
- 1 tech talk delivered
- 1 architecture proposal written

#### Quarter 3 (January – March): Proving ground

**Focus:** act like a Tech Lead in real conditions, so we both know whether the role fits.

**Goals:**
1. Step in as acting Tech Lead while I am on leave (2 weeks)
2. Lead hiring for one open position
3. Take full technical decision ownership on one subsystem

**Actions:**

| Action | Support I will give | Timeline | How we measure it |
|---|---|---|---|
| Act as Tech Lead for 2 weeks during my annual leave | Full prep sessions before and debrief after | Month 7 | Zero escalations during acting period |
| Own the hiring pipeline for one new backend engineer | Partnership with HR, interview training | Month 8 | 1 successful hire, from JD to offer |
| Own technical decisions for the payment subsystem | Trust + monthly light-touch review | Ongoing | Decisions stick without reversal |

**Success metrics at the end of Q3:**
- Acting period goes smoothly, team gives positive feedback in my return 1:1s
- Hire closed or deep in final rounds
- No reversed technical decisions on the owned subsystem

#### Quarter 4 (April – June): Transition

**Focus:** formal role change and the first 90 days in the new title.

**Goals:**
1. Formal Tech Lead role change
2. Deliver a 90-day plan for the new role
3. Hand off the IC work cleanly

**Actions:**

| Action | Support I will give | Timeline | How we measure it |
|---|---|---|---|
| Tech Lead role conversation with skip-level | Prep Alex for the conversation, sit in if wanted | Month 10 | Role change approved |
| Write a 90-day plan for the Tech Lead role | Coaching sessions, review the draft | Month 10–11 | Plan shared with the team |
| Announce and transition | Communication support, town-hall moment | Month 10 | Team briefed and onboard |
| Hand off current IC ownership to the team | Documentation sprint, pair handoffs | Month 11 | No surprises land on new TL's desk |

### Resources

- **Books to work through together:** "The Manager's Path" (Camille Fournier), "Staff Engineer" (Will Larson) — I will join Alex for a monthly book discussion over coffee
- **Course:** "Leading Through Influence" online workshop, budget approved
- **Mentorship:** Sandun from platform team agreed to act as a Tech Lead mentor, 30 minutes every 2 weeks
- **Conference:** I will nominate Alex for one internal engineering summit speaking slot in Q2

### Check-in schedule

- **Weekly:** 1:1s, where we always keep 5 minutes at the end for "how is the plan going?"
- **Monthly:** 30-minute progress review against the quarterly actions
- **Quarterly:** formal assessment with me, and sharing progress with skip-level
- **Ad hoc:** if Alex ever wants to re-scope the plan, they can — I will not treat that as failure

### What I am committing to as Alex's manager

- I will not take this plan and forget it in a drawer
- I will give Alex real stretch opportunities, not busy work
- I will give Alex honest feedback, quickly, in both directions
- I will advocate for Alex in the rooms Alex is not in
- If the plan is not working, I will tell Alex before I tell anyone else

## Career Plan 2 — Jordan Martinez

**Current role:** Mid-Level Engineer
**Target role:** Senior Engineer
**Timeline:** 18 months

### Career aspirations (in Jordan's own words)

Jordan is less certain about the long-term path than Alex is, and that is completely fine. A plan built on honest uncertainty is better than a plan built on false confidence. When I asked Jordan about the long-term path, this is what came out:

- **Short-term (12–18 months):** become a Senior Engineer
- **Medium-term (3 years):** either Tech Lead or deep technical specialist — Jordan genuinely does not know yet and does not want to pretend
- **Long-term:** "I want to do work I am proud of and still have time for my family."

That last line matters. It tells me the plan should not push Jordan into a 60-hour grind. It should build skills in the hours Jordan already has.

### Current state — honest assessment

Jordan is more capable than Jordan realises, and that gap between actual ability and self-perception is the single biggest thing we need to work on. The technical gaps are smaller than the confidence gap.

**Strengths:**

| Skill | Level | Evidence |
|---|---|---|
| Problem solving | Proficient | Solved the caching puzzle last month after two seniors had looked at it |
| Learning agility | Strong | Picked up the new testing framework faster than Sam |
| Collaboration | Strong | Jordan is the person Casey asks first |
| Debugging | Proficient | Calm and methodical under pressure |

**Gaps for the Senior bar:**

| Skill | Level | Why it matters |
|---|---|---|
| System design | Developing | Senior engineers need to be able to propose, not just implement |
| Technical depth in one area | Developing | Jordan is a generalist right now; Seniors usually have one area of real expertise |
| Independence | Developing | Jordan often waits for confirmation when Jordan already knows the answer |
| Code review as a teaching moment | New | Jordan approves PRs but does not yet mentor through them |

**Confidence (not a technical gap, but the biggest one):**
Jordan second-guesses sound decisions. This is the thing I want to work on in every 1:1, not through a course or a book, but through small wins.

### Development plan

18 months is a longer runway than Alex has, and that is deliberate. Jordan's path benefits from more time and less pressure. The plan has 3 phases of 6 months each.

#### Phase 1 (Months 1–6): Foundation — Build the technical base

**Focus:** close the system design gap and own some features end-to-end so confidence starts to rise.

**Actions:**

| Action | Support I will give | Timeline | How we measure it |
|---|---|---|---|
| Complete a system design course | Learning budget, pairing with Alex on exercises | Month 1–3 | Course finished, Jordan explains the key patterns back to me |
| Own 2 small features end-to-end (no pairing) | Ticket scoping help, ready to unblock | Month 2–6 | 2 features shipped, no pairing sessions |
| Pair with Alex on the notification service refactor | Scheduled sessions, not ad hoc | Month 4–6 | Refactor shipped, Jordan contributed design, not just code |
| Read "Designing Data-Intensive Applications" chapters 1–4 | Monthly discussion with me | Month 1–6 | Monthly 15-minute chat |

**Success metrics at end of Phase 1:**
- 2 features delivered solo
- System design course completed
- 1 architecture discussion where Jordan contributed an opinion, not just a question

#### Phase 2 (Months 7–12): Independence — Stretch into senior behaviours

**Focus:** reduce dependence on me and the seniors. Jordan should start being the person others go to, not only the person who goes to others.

**Actions:**

| Action | Support I will give | Timeline | How we measure it |
|---|---|---|---|
| Lead one feature as primary owner | Check-in only at milestone reviews | Month 7–9 | Feature shipped with minimal intervention |
| Review junior code (Casey) as a teaching activity | Review guidance, model the first few | Monthly | Casey cites Jordan's reviews as helpful |
| Contribute to architecture discussions actively | Pre-meeting prep sessions with me | Ongoing | Jordan raises at least one substantive point per meeting |
| Pick one area to specialise in (Jordan's choice) | Learning budget + time carve-out | Month 7 | Area chosen by end of month 7 |

**Success metrics at end of Phase 2:**
- 1 feature led and shipped
- 3 reviews of Casey's code that Casey rates "helpful"
- Clear specialisation picked and communicated to the team
- Jordan speaks up in architecture meetings without being asked

#### Phase 3 (Months 13–18): Senior readiness — demonstrate it

**Focus:** show that Jordan is already operating at the Senior level on the things that matter most. Make the promotion case easy to make.

**Actions:**

| Action | Support I will give | Timeline | How we measure it |
|---|---|---|---|
| Mentor Casey through a full feature cycle | Monthly check-ins with both of you | Month 13–15 | Casey grows visibly, Jordan gains mentoring reps |
| Propose and drive one technical improvement | Review of the proposal, sponsor in meetings | Month 14 | Proposal approved and implemented |
| Own an incident response as IC on-call | Shadow opportunity on the next P1 | When one happens | Incident handled without escalation |
| Present a tech talk on your specialisation area | Deck review, practice runs | Month 17 | Talk delivered to the team |

**Success metrics at end of Phase 3:**
- Successful mentorship relationship with Casey
- 1 technical proposal shipped
- At least one incident handled as on-call
- Promotion case ready to write by month 17

### Resources

- **Course:** "System Design Fundamentals" (budget approved)
- **Book:** "Designing Data-Intensive Applications" — I will read along and we will discuss monthly
- **Pair programming:** rotating schedule with Alex, Sam, and Morgan — one week each per month, so Jordan sees different styles
- **Book club:** I will suggest the team runs a technical book club so learning does not feel isolating

### Check-in schedule

- **Weekly:** 1:1s, with a standing item: "one thing you were proud of this week"
- **Bi-monthly:** skills self-assessment, so Jordan tracks own progress against the gap list
- **Quarterly:** formal career conversation — review the plan, adjust if needed
- **Ad hoc:** Jordan can bring up the plan any time

### What I am committing to as Jordan's manager

- I will celebrate the small wins in public, because confidence is built through visible recognition
- I will not rescue Jordan on features where struggling is part of the growth
- I will give honest feedback early, so Jordan never has to guess where they stand
- I will not push Jordan into a path Jordan has not chosen
- When Jordan is ready for promotion, I will fight for it

---

## Closing note

Both plans are deliberately realistic, not aspirational. I would rather Alex hit 90% of a real plan than 40% of a heroic one, and I would rather Jordan gain 18 months of steady confidence than be rushed and burn out. Careers are long. The best thing I can do as a manager is protect the pace that lets someone keep growing for years, not just quarters.
