## Additional Notes

The sections below are commitments I made to myself as Alex's and Jordan's manager before writing the plans. They do not change the plan structure above, but they shape how I execute it day to day.

### Commitments as Alex's manager
- I will not take this plan and forget it in a drawer
- I will give Alex real stretch opportunities, not busy work
- I will give Alex honest feedback, quickly, in both directions
- I will advocate for Alex in the rooms Alex is not in
- If the plan is not working, I will tell Alex before I tell anyone else

### Commitments as Jordan's manager
- I will celebrate the small wins in public, because confidence is built through visible recognition
- I will not rescue Jordan on features where struggling is part of the growth
- I will give honest feedback early, so Jordan never has to guess where they stand
- I will not push Jordan into a path Jordan has not chosen
- When Jordan is ready for promotion, I will fight for it

### Note on the two different timelines

Alex gets 12 months, Jordan gets 18. This is not because Jordan is weaker — Jordan is more capable than Jordan realises. The longer runway is a deliberate choice to protect pace and build real confidence. Careers are long, and the best thing I can do as a manager is protect the pace that lets someone keep growing for years, not just quarters.
