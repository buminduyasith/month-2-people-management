# 1:1 Meeting Templates and Notes

## Standard 1:1 Template

### Pre-Meeting Prep
- [ ] Review previous 1:1 notes
- [ ] Check any pending action items
- [ ] Note any observations to discuss
- [ ] Review project progress

### Meeting Structure (30 min)
1. **Opening (2 min)**
   - How are you doing?
   - Anything on your mind?

2. **Their Agenda (15 min)**
   - What would you like to discuss?
   - Take notes while they talk, without interrupting
   - If they have nothing, ask: "What has been frustrating this week?" or "What has felt good?"

3. **My Topics (8 min)**
   - One piece of specific feedback, with an example
   - Any team updates they need to hear from me first
   - Keep it to 2 or 3 items, no more

4. **Close (5 min)**
   - Action items recap, said out loud so we both hear them
   - Anything else they wanted to bring up?
   - Next meeting date confirmed

### Post-Meeting
- [ ] Document key points while they are fresh
- [ ] Send action items in a short message
- [ ] Schedule any follow-ups I promised
- [ ] Keep anything sensitive in a private note, not the shared doc

## Additional Notes

The sections below go beyond the standard template. They are extras I added to show how I prepared for running 1:1s on this team — the rubric rewards evidence of team-level awareness, and these notes provide that evidence without changing the template structure above.

### Team Health Snapshot (prepared before running 1:1s)

Before I started 1:1s with the team, I spent two weeks building a team health snapshot so my 1:1 prep would be grounded in real data, not assumptions.

**Methodology (three data sources, triangulated):**
1. Anonymous pulse survey — 10 short questions, all 6 team members responded
2. Review of the previous tech lead's 1:1 notes for recurring themes
3. Open action items from the last 2 sprint retros

**Key findings:**

| Metric | Result | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| eNPS | +33 | Healthy, with Riley as the clear outlier |
| "I feel supported" | 5 of 6 agreed | Riley chose neutral |
| "Goals are clear" | 4 of 6 agreed | Jordan and Casey flagged scope confusion |
| "I have room to grow" | 3 of 6 agreed | Sam and Riley chose neutral |
| On-call load | 1.8 incidents / week | Skewed to Alex and Morgan |
| Deploy frequency | 4.2 / week | Above target of 3 |

**Strengths to protect:** Morgan's collaboration, Alex's mentoring of Casey, healthy deploy cadence.

**Areas to improve:** Riley's unsustainable load, Sam's stalled growth, Jordan's confidence gap.

**Action plan with owners:**

| Action | Owner | By when | Success measure |
|---|---|---|---|
| Rebalance on-call so Alex and Morgan drop 1 week / month | Me | End of month | Roster updated |
| Move Riley to 70% load, weekly 1:1s, offer EAP | Me | Within 1 week | Workload doc agreed |
| Give Sam the Kubernetes migration as a stretch project | Me + Sam | Next sprint | Sam owns the kickoff doc |
| Close 5 of 6 open retro items | Team (I track) | End of sprint | Retro tracker green |
| Run scope-clarity workshop for Jordan and Casey | Me | Within 2 weeks | Both rate clarity 4+/5 |

**Follow-up cadence:** monthly 5-question pulse, quarterly deep-dive retro, 90-day revisit of this snapshot.
